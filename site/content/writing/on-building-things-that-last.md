There's a particular satisfaction in making something that outlasts the moment it was built for. Not everything needs to — most things probably shouldn't. But occasionally you find yourself working on something where durability matters, and the constraints that come with that shift everything about how you think.

I've been turning this over while working on a recent project. The temptation is always to optimize for now: current tooling, current assumptions, current team. But current is a moving target...
